Interpretation of the data columns - (Names of Features and what they imply)¶

imdb_id -- id for each movies from the database

popularity -- how much the movie's titles page has been visited that year

budget -- how much went into the production of the movie

revenue -- how mucch was made from the sale of the movie

director -- movie director

runtime -- lenght of movie in minutes

genres -- literary grnee of the movie

production_companies -- company responsible for the movie production

release_date -- the exact date the movie was released for public viewing

vote_count -- this has to do with a movie's rating

vote_average -- this is similiar to a vote_count

release_year -- the year he movie was released for public viewing

Limitations¶

The following are challenges encountered and limitaions posed while working on this data:¶
  1. The revenue of about 2,000 movies revenue nad budget recrord were not reported which posed a challenge in deciding whtheer to keep the rows these movies belong to. This is becuase budget and revenues are two important features I settled on for this analysis

  1. The data was quite sufficint feature wise in driving my analysis as my analysis was concentrated on profitability insights from the data. However the missing values and NaN columns actually made the data quite tricky to deal with than envisaged.

  2. What could be considered a limitation while working with the data was filling rows that had none of this financial records with the mean of the respective columns. Becuase I am dealing with data especially one reporting for independent initiatives (movie productions in this case), I had to take extra care in filling rows with mean values.

The challenge with this is that doing this can eventually misrepresent even the well reported datum or records in our body of data such as offsetting the mean values and other statistical values; with greater implication when performing correlation or regression analysis on our data.
